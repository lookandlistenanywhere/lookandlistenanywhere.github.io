Look and Listen Anywhere

Jackie Lin *, Chaitanya Amballa *

University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

Abstract

Sound and vision provide complementary cues for perceiving our environments. While visual cues offer localized, fine-grained details, audio offers broader, spatially diffuse insights, including information outside the camera's view. Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs) have successfully synthesized novel scenes by optimizing a volumetric function. In this work, we extend the capabilities of NeRFs to generate both novel visual scenes and corresponding binaural audio. Specifically, given a video recording of an indoor scene and a set of binaural room impulse responses (BRIRs) captured at known locations, our goal is to synthesize a video with binaural audio for any audio source placed anywhere within the scene, following any trajectory. We propose integrating visual cues into an audio-specific NeRF, enabling the generation of synchronized audio-visual content. Additionally, we leverage the SoundSpaces to synthesize high-quality audio-visual datasets. Our approach demonstrates promising results in generating dynamic, spatially accurate audio-visual scenes.
