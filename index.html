Look and Listen Anywhere

Jackie Lin *, Chaitanya Amballa *

University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

Abstract

Vision and sound are fundamental to human perception, particularly for interpreting environments. Visual cues provide localized, detailed information, while audio offers broader, more spatially diffuse cues. Audio contains information beyond what is available visually in two key ways: 1) sound from sources outside the camera’s field of view or behind visual obstructions can still be perceived, and 2) the spectral and temporal characteristics of audio reveal physical properties of the scene’s geometry, both inside and outside the visual range. These audio cues help convey details about the scene’s geometry, size, topology, surfaces.
